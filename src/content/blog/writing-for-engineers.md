---
title: "Writing for engineers (without pretending you have extra hours)"
description: "Why good notes beat heroic memory—and how to keep publishing lightweight."
pubDate: 2026-03-02
---

Most engineering progress is invisible: a decision in a design doc, a tradeoff in a PR description, a postmortem that prevents the next outage. Writing is how you scale your judgment beyond the people in the room.

The hard part is not talent; it is friction. If publishing feels like a ceremony, you will not do it. Keep a single place for drafts—could be a repo, a folder, or a blog like this one—and ship small. A page that explains one idea clearly is more valuable than a manifesto you never finish.

When you write, optimize for the reader who will skim. Start with the decision or claim, then evidence, then caveats. Link to code and dashboards when you can. And remember: the goal is not to sound smart; it is to make the next person faster.
