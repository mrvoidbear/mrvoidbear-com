---
title: "Shipping with constraints"
description: "A practical frame for delivery when time, scope, and quality pull in different directions."
pubDate: 2026-02-14
---

Every team claims to prioritize, but the proof is what ships. Constraints are not the enemy—they are the forcing function that turns opinions into outcomes.

When scope is fixed, invest in the riskiest unknowns first. Spike the integration, validate the data model, or prove the performance story before polishing edges nobody will touch in v1.

When time is fixed, cut vertically: a thin slice that works end-to-end beats a wide surface of half-finished pieces. Users forgive roughness if the core loop is credible; they rarely forgive “almost ready” forever.

When quality is non-negotiable, make it explicit what “quality” means for this release: correctness, resilience, accessibility, operability. Write those criteria down and test against them. The constraint isn’t “no bugs”; it is “no surprises in the areas we said mattered.”
