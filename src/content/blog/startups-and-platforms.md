---
title: "Startups and platforms: two different games"
description: "Speed and leverage look similar from a distance; they optimize for different failures."
pubDate: 2026-01-20
---

Startups often win by learning quickly: tight feedback loops, prototypes that embarrass you, and a willingness to rewrite when reality disagrees with the plan. The failure mode is building something nobody wants—so the system should optimize for discovery.

Platform work often wins by not surprising people: stable contracts, predictable rollouts, and tooling that makes the right path easy. The failure mode is fragility at scale—so the system should optimize for change without chaos.

The mistake is mixing the two without naming which game you are playing. A startup team can borrow platform discipline when it protects learning (observability, feature flags). A platform team can borrow startup urgency when users are blocked (time-boxed migrations, crisp OKRs).

If you are unsure, ask: what would embarrass us if it broke tomorrow? That answer tells you whether you are buying speed or buying leverage—and what you must write down so the next person does not have to relearn it the hard way.
